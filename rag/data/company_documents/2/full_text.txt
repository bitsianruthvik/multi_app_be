1. INTRODUCTION TO ONCARYVA™ & COMPANY MISSION

Oncaryva™ is our flagship targeted therapy for FGFR2-fusion–positive cholangiocarcinoma, an area with limited treatment options and significant patient need. Our mission is to bring precision oncology solutions to underserved cancers while maintaining the highest standards of scientific integrity.

As an oncology sales representative, your role is not “selling medicine”—
your role is enabling doctors to make the most informed, evidence-based decisions for their patients.

⸻

2. DISEASE BACKGROUND: CHOLANGIOCARCINOMA & FGFR BIOLOGY

Cholangiocarcinoma Overview
	•	Rare, aggressive cancer originating in the bile ducts
	•	Incidence: increasing globally due to improved diagnosis
	•	Median overall survival after first-line therapy: 11–14 months
	•	FGFR2 fusions occur in 10–16% of intrahepatic cases

What FGFR2 Fusions Do
	•	Create ligand-independent activation
	•	Trigger MAPK, PI3K–AKT, and STAT pathways
	•	Drive uncontrolled tumor proliferation
	•	Associated with unique biology—more indolent but more metastatic to lymphatics

Understanding FGFR biology is foundational. Oncologists expect reps to know the science.

⸻

3. PRODUCT DEEP DIVE – ONCARYVA™ (ROVELCITIB MESYLATE)

What It Is
	•	Oral, once-daily targeted inhibitor
	•	Dual activity: FGFR2 (primary) + VEGFR3 (secondary)
	•	High selectivity: 18× over FGFR1 → better metabolic safety profile
	•	Eliminates reliance on IV therapy for targeted lines

Formulation
	•	40 mg film-coated tablets
	•	Linear PK, minimal food effect
	•	Predictable interpatient exposure

7. SAFETY PROFILE & AE MANAGEMENT

Common AEs
	•	Hyperphosphatemia (38%)
	•	Dry mouth (27%)
	•	Fatigue (22%)
	•	Nail changes (14%)

Serious AEs
	•	Stomatitis – 4%
	•	RPED – 1.1%
	•	ALT/AST elevation – 3%

Monitoring
	•	Phosphate: weekly x 4 weeks
	•	LFT: every 2 weeks x 2 months
	•	Eye exam: every 8 weeks

8. COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE & POSITIONING

Key competitors: pemigatinib, infigratinib, futibatinib

Oncaryva™ advantage points:
	•	Lower hyperphosphatemia (38% vs 56%)
	•	Once-daily vs cyclical dosing
	•	Lower discontinuation rate
	•	Stronger real-world evidence support

Positioning Line:

“Oncaryva™ offers strong efficacy with predictable, manageable safety that fits real-world practice.”

9. COMPLIANCE BOUNDARIES

You may NOT:
	•	Promote off-label use
	•	Compare competitors using unpublished data
	•	Suggest superiority without referencing a source
	•	Discuss price without the approved pathway
	•	Share unapproved slides or WhatsApp charts

You MUST:
	•	Stick to PI-approved claims
	•	Use balanced risk–benefit discussion
	•	Maintain scientific tone

13. HANDLING OBJECTIONS

“I’ve seen hyperphosphatemia issues with FGFR inhibitors.”

“Yes, that’s common for the class. Oncaryva™ shows a lower incidence (38%), and in RWD-2025, most cases were Grade 1–2 and manageable.”

“My patients don’t adhere to oral targeted therapies well.”

“That’s a valid concern. The once-daily regimen has shown better adherence in the real-world dataset.”

“I don’t routinely test for FGFR2 fusions.”

“Would it be helpful if I share a list of local labs offering fast-turnaround NGS?”
